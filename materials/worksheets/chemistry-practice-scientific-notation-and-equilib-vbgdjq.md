### Section 1: Scientific Notation

1.  **Multiple Choice:** Which of the following numbers is NOT written in correct scientific notation?
    a) $5.67 \times 10^3$
    b) $0.88 \times 10^{-2}$
    c) $1.00 \times 10^7$
    d) $9.99 \times 10^0$

2.  For the number $7.25 \times 10^{-6}$:
    a) What is the coefficient ($N$)?
    b) What is the exponent ($x$)?
    c) Is the original number represented by this scientific notation extremely large or extremely small?

3.  Convert the following numbers from standard form to scientific notation:
    a) $87,600,000$
    b) $0.000000105$
    c) $4,500$
    d) $0.0032$
    e) $602,200,000,000,000,000,000,000$ (Avogadro's number)

4.  Convert the following numbers from scientific notation to standard form:
    a) $1.2 \times 10^4$
    b) $9.81 \times 10^{-3}$
    c) $5.0 \times 10^0$
    d) $3.14159 \times 10^6$
    e) $7.00 \times 10^{-7}$

5.  Explain in your own words why scientific notation is useful in chemistry. Provide an example of a chemical quantity that would benefit from being expressed in scientific notation.
